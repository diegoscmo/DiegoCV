My Trajectory

A brief timeline of what I have been doing so far (I hope to get into more details of relevant projects as I further develop this page).

  • July 2009

    Mechanical Engineering 101

    Started a Mechanical Engineering course at UDESC (Universidade do Estado de Santa Catarina). Understanding how things works and the habit of taking things apart as a kid were my two main drivers towards engineering.

  • March 2010

    Velociraptor Baja-SAE

    I began participating in the Baja-SAE Velociraptor team. The focus of the project is to design and build an off-road vehicle for competitions between universities. Good place to learn a lot about automotive engineering, project managing and teamwork.

  • December 2010

    Summer Work/Travel Exchange

    Since I was a kid I have always enjoyed the video games, since at that time none of the current games were in Portuguese, my older brother gave me an English dictionary. With that, I learned how to write and read in English around 10. Going abroad was a fast way to finally practice and learn how to speak. I have spent three months working in a restaurant at Nascar Sports Grille in Orlando/Florida.

  • September 2011

    Own Baja Project

    After learning a lot in the Baja team, I have decided to design and build my own. Together with my older brother (also a Mechanical Engineer), we did just that. We have designed an off-road vehicle from scratch and build in our garage, my brother used the project as his Graduation Thesis later on, we took almost a year and a half of weekends to get it running (Hope to go into more depth of the designing process here later on).

  • December 2013

    Thesis, Particle Swarm Truss Optimization

    Time to work on my thesis! I fell in love with optimization the moment that my professor started explaining it to me. Since I had a lot of trouble designing the Truss-like chassis of our Baja Project, Truss optimization was a natural choice for a thesis. I had a lot of fun working on this and learned a lot about Optimization and Finite Elements Method, which are my two main areas of interest together with Structural calculations.

  • February 2014

    Internship, WEG - Wind Power

    I was lucky enough to get a position in the brand new Wind Power department at WEG. Wind Turbines are an awesome product to learn and work with. I landed a position in Supply Chain and did a little bit of everything in there as an intern.

  • July 2014

    Graduation and Job!

    Finally, I have graduated! In the same month, I got an effective position at WEG, still working with Wind Turbines but now more focused on Developing Suppliers for components of a new Wind Turbine (the AGW3.3). I am still exponentially learning about all sorts of critical components of a Gearless Wind Turbine, to name a few: Pitch System, Slipring, Slewing Bearings, Cooling System, Pitch and Yaw Gearboxes, Structural Castings, and the list goes on…

  • August 2015

    Began my Master's Degree

    Began a Masters course in Project, Analysis and Optimization of Mechanical Systems. Since I am in the first semester with just a couple of classes each week I still do not know what I will be working on for a thesis. I am hoping to use this knowledge in a more technical position later on.

  • September 2015

    Nemo Project

    Now I am together with two other engineers to design and build a Hydrofoil Bicycle. We are doing this mostly for fun right now and we hope to get that done in a year, I will try to record part of the process in here.
