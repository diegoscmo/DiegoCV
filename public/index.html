My Trajectory

A brief timeline of what I have been doing so far. (I hope to get into more details of relevant projects as I further develop this page)

  • July 2009

    Mechanical Engineering 101

    Started a Mechanical Engineering course at UDESC (Universidade do Estado de Santa Catarina). I will save you the story of the kid that liked to take things apart.. It is true in my case, but all other engineers already tell this one.

  • March 2010

    Velociraptor Baja-SAE

    I began participating in the Baja-SAE Velociraptor team. The focus of the project is to design and build an off-road vehicle for competitions between universities. Really good place to learn a lot about automotive engineering, project managing and team-work.

  • December 2010

    Summer Work/Travel Exchange

    Since I was a kid I’ve always enjoyed the old video-games, my older brother gave me an English dictionary so I could understand what I had to do in the games, with that I learned how to write and read in English around 10. Going abroad was a fast way to finally learn how to speak. I’ve spent three months working in a restaurant at Nascar Sports Grille in Orlando/Florida.

  • September 2011

    Own Baja Project

    After learning a lot in the Baja team, I’ve decided to design and build my own. Together with my older brother (also Mec. Engineer) we did just that. We’ve designed it from scratch and build in our garage, my brother used the project as his Thesis later on, we took almost a year and a half to finish (only weekends). Several years have passed and our mom is still mad at us. (Hope to go into more depth of the designing process here later on)

  • December 2013

    Thesis, Particle Swarm Truss Optimization

    Time to work on my thesis! I fell in love with optimization the moment that my professor started explaning it to me. Since I had a lot of trouble designing the Truss-like chassis of our Baja Project, Truss optimization was a natural choise. I had a lot of fun working on this and also learned a lot about Optimization and Finite Elements Method.

  • February 2014

    Internship, WEG - Wind Power

    I was lucky enough to get a position in the brand new Wind Power department at WEG. Wind Turbines are an awesome product to learn and work with. I’ve got a position in Supply Chain and did a little bit of everything in there as an intern.

  • July 2014

    Graduation and Job!

    Finally I’ve graduated! In the same month I got an effective position at WEG, still working with Wind Turbines but now more focused on Developing Suppliers for components of a new Wind Turbine (the AGW3.3).

  • August 2015

    Began my Master's Degree

    Began a Masters course in Project, Analysis and Optimization of Mechanical Systems. Never thought that I would miss college. I am hoping to use this knowledge in a more technical position later on.

  • September 2015

    Nemo Project

    Now I am together with two other crazy engineers to design and build a Hydrofoil Bycicle. We hope to get that done in a year, I will try to record part of the process in here.
